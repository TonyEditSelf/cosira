4. Chroma Contrast Palettes (Quiet + Loud)

Idea: Same hue & lightness, different chroma.

Example

Base: L 70, C 0.05, H 210

Accent: L 70, C 0.25, H 210

Result

Accent pops without brightness changes

Very modern, very controlled


8. Emotional Lightness Palettes

Idea: Encode emotion via L, not hue.

Examples

Anxiety → high L contrast

Calm → mid-L clustering

Luxury → low L + low C

Playful → mid-L + high C

This is difficult in RGB, easy in OKLCH.



21. Broken-Lightness Palettes

Idea: Intentionally violate smooth lightness ramps.

L jumps while H stays close

Creates tension and rhythm

Great for experimental UI, editorial spreads

Why it works: the eye notices structure, not chaos.


22. Chroma-Only Gradients

Idea: Same L and H, chroma fades to zero.

Color → gray → color

Perfect for focus/defocus states

Feels subtle but intentional



23. Near-Neutral Hue Drift

Idea: Ultra-low chroma with slow hue drift.

C = 0.01–0.04

H shifts 30–60°

Feels neutral, but never dead.


24. Perceptual “Shadow” Palettes

Idea: Shadows are cooler + lower chroma, not just darker.

Highlights: higher L, warmer H

Shadows: lower L, cooler H

Much closer to how real light behaves.



25. Inverted Contrast Palettes

Idea: High chroma at low lightness, low chroma at high lightness.

Saturated darks

Washed-out lights

Creates cinematic, night-scene vibes.


33. Semantic Chroma Encoding

Idea: Meaning = chroma, not hue.

Same hue everywhere

Urgency, importance, focus via C only

Extremely clean for enterprise UI.
