Built a mood-constrained color palette generator for my brand creation tool.
Instead of spinning a random hex wheel, you pick a mood and the generator works within that mood's hue range, chroma limits, and lightness bounds. Every result feels intentional, not accidental.
8 moods to explore:
🌿 Warm & Earthy — terracotta, rust, sand, sage
🌊 Ocean & Calm — deep navy, teal, seafoam, mist
⚡ Bold & Electric — vivid, high-energy, saturated
🌆 Dusk & Moody — deep purples, mauve, dusty rose
🌸 Fresh & Minimal — soft pastels, airy, light
✦ Luxe & Dark — ebony, deep gold, midnight
🔮 Neon Cyber — acid greens, hot pinks, glows
❄️ Nordic Frost — ice blue, white birch, pale grey
A few things that make it actually useful:
→ Harmony modes (analogous, complementary, triadic) that constrain hue relationships across the whole palette
→ Contrast lock — guarantee every color passes WCAG AA or AAA before it even appears
→ Lock individual colors you like, regenerate the rest
→ Tonal range explorer for any generated color
→ Full generation history so you never lose a palette you liked
Built with chroma.js + OKLCH color space, which handles perceptual uniformity way better than HSL for this kind of work.
Randomness within rules beats pure randomness every time.
